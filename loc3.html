Yau Tsim Mong

Yau Tsim Mong is the most visited area by teenagers and youngsters in general because of its low price food and goods. You'll see a lot of fashionable people around with their coolest fits. You can pretty much do anything here with the varse choices of shopping items including makeup (good to cover for you undereye bag from all the latenight studying!), food, anime merch, kpop merch, and more youth's favourites.

Including West Kowloon, Sham Shui Po and Tsim Sha Tsui area, these are really nice place to visit for shopping and a good view.

West Kowloon

M+ Art Museum

M+

M+ visit guide
Opening Hour:

Tues-Thurs, weekends: 10:00-18:00

Fri: 10:00-22:00

Mon: Closed

Price:

Free entry. For Special Exhibition and general admission, please check their official website. With concession (present student id), usually it will be below 100HKD.

Address:

M+, West Kowloon Cultural District, 38 Museum Drive, Kowloon

How to get there:

MTR Kowloon Station, exit C1 or D1, take the escalator to 1/F and follow the signs through ELEMENTS to the Artist Square Bridge on 2/F, Metal Zone to reach WestK. The walk is approximately ten to fifteen minutes.

About:

If you're one of the artsy peeps, M+ is your dream and home. M+ is asia's first global museum of contemporary visual culture. Get ready to fully open all your senses because it is so much more than just a regular art museum. The 65,000sq meter venue is designed by renowned Swiss architecture firm Herzog & de Meuron. Inside, the building features 17,000sq meter of exhibition space across 33 galleries, and houses various facilities and public spaces, including a rooftop garden, research centre, multimedia library, restaurants, cinemas, and the Found Space, which caters to major installations. Exhibitions at the museum will cover themes of architecture and design, post-war art, conceptual art, installation art and much more.

The exhibition changes all the time so make sure to pay constant visits for new inspirations!

Sham Shui Po

Apliu Street

Apliu Street

Opening Hour:

Around 10am to 8pm

Price:

Depends on your shopping decision.

Address:

Apliu Street, Shum Shui Po

How to get there:

MTR Sham Shui Po Station, exit at A2 or C2. It is right outside of the exit.

About:

Apliu Street, also called Electronic street, is a street market full of electronic goodies. If you're looking for cheap electronics, such as secondhand smart devices, simcard, minifan, batteries, literally anything you need in this digital world, you can find it here.

As shown in the pictures, yes, you can find vintage ccd cameras which is trending right now for retro photography effect!

Tips: Always bargain with the stall owner! You will get a much lower and reasonable price.

Be aware of thiefs, hold your bags tight. Better safe than sorry! It is quite crowded and you should be alertful of theft.

Tsim Sha Tsui

Victoria Harbour

Victoria Harbour

Opening Hour:

all day, outdoor

Price:

Free. Unless you go shopping.

Address:

Victoria Harbour, Tsim Sha Tsui

How to get there:

Any exit at MTR Tsim Sha Tsui Station. It will take around 10-15min to walk to the seaside.

About:

Victoria Harbour is probably the most famous Hong Kong landmark. It has one of the world's top night view. Sitting by the seaside along the avenue, feeling the cool breeze and listening to the waves, this is where you find your love pounding intensely for Hong Kong.

Nearby, you may visit the Avenue of Stars, Hong Kong Art Museum, K11, Hong Kong Cultural Centre, and more.

As students, it is a nice place to chill and gather with friends, also very exciting to go shopping and wander around the beauty of Hong Kong.

Shopping malls may be expensive and too classy for your age, however there are lots of inspiring designer shops and restaurants to visit!
