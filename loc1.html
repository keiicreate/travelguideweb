Hong Kong Island East

Hong Kong Island East is one of the area with the most residency and schools. As someone who grew up in this area, I would love to share my neighborhood best visits to you all!

  • North Point
  • Causeway Bay

North Point

Chan Kee Fishball

Chan Kee Fishball

Opening Hour:

11am-10pm

Price:

10-20HKD

Address:

Kiu Fai Mansion, King's Rd, North Point

How to get there:

MTR North Point Station, exit A1. Cross the road and you'll see Chan Kee (right beside Sunbeam Theatre).

About:

My oh my, I grew up here and Chan Kee is my favourite snack shop. They have a secret sauce that makes their food so special and delicious!

I always come here for a few hunger bites after my bus ride afterschool. If you would like to, feel free to walk down the street to the sea side (right next to Harbour North) and enjoy the view!

Big question: Are you team Siu Mai or team Fishball? I am team Siu Mai!

Hoi Ka Sichuan Boiled Fish

Hoi Ka Sichuan Boiled Fish

Opening Hour:

12pm-10pm

Price:

50-200HKD

Address:

483-487 Ground Floor shop B, King's Road, North Point

How to get there:

MTR North Point Station, exit A1. Cross the road and turn right. Keep walking straight, cross the road again and you'll see it on your left.

About:

Best Sichuan dish restaurant in the neigbourhood!!! If you enjoy spicy Sichuan style chinese food, definitely come fill your stomach here at Hoi Ka.

Their signature dish is the Sichuan boiled fish with picked cabbage (Suancai Yu). I also recommend Spicy Chicken (Laziji) and their black sugar mochi!

Tak Hing Lung Tofu

Tak Hing Lung Tofu

Opening Hour:

7am-7pm

Price:

10-20HKD

Address:

1 Marble Road, North Point

How to get there:

MTR North Point Station, exit A1. Walk straight, cross the road twice and turn left into Marble Road, you'll see an alley with market stalls on both sides. Stay on the right hand side pavement, walk for a few more steps and you should see it on your right.

About:

Craving for tofu? For Bao? For fresh Soybean Milk? Even Tofu Pudding? Visit Tak Hing Lung for authentic tofu goodness. It has been open for over 60 years!

This is my go-to supper place afterschool, and before my tutoring centre classes on weekends. It is extremely affordable and real, owned by local old grannies, they love to chit-chat with customers.

Definitely try their Tofu pudding, it's so soft and rich in soybean. I also recommend their soybean milk, it is made freshly everyday in the morning! If you live nearby like me, you can buy some fresh tofu and bring home for cooking.

Causewaybay

He She Eat

He She Eat

Opening Hour:

10am-10pm

Price:

50-200HKD

Address:

2/F, Causeway Place, 2-10 Great George St, Causeway Bay

How to get there:

MTR Causeway Bay Station, exit E. Cross the road and you'll see an entry to Causeway Place with both stairs and lift. Get to the 2/F and you'll see the restaurant.

About:

A really nice and affordable western food cafe/restaurant. Located at the busy district of Causewaybay, it has a nice window view of the Great Geore Street. As a student, I always visit Causeway Bay afteschool as it is the closest shopping area from my highschool, and I believe a lot of you students go to school nearby too.

Nearby, we have IKEA where I always go to sit at their sofa area and window shop with their furnitures, as well as enjoying their great food service, and also Windsor House Shopping Mall including a cinema! Just a few meters away you can visit SOGO, Hysan Place and Lee Gardens for shopping cravings.

Highly recommend their afternoon tea set for under 80HKD! They also have really well-presented dinner starting from 90HKD which is pretty affordable!

As a kpop lover and foodie, if you are one too, you'll understand how important it is to take nice pics of my photocards and dolls with my food. I can assure you He She Eat has one of the best food presentation for photo snapping!
